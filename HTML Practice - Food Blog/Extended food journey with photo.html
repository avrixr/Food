Food Journey

My name is Avrix. That little picture down there is me. Happy as can absolutely be You're probably wondering why I'm making this. Well, I want to express how important food has been through the journey of my life. Join or don't, but I'm having fun. Click the "Fine Dining" dropdown to continue.

The journey began back in Kindergarten. Some of the earliest food memories I have are:

  • My mom's fried Chicken straight out of the pot
  • My mom's smothered pork chops (Or as I used to call it, "Chicken")
  • Bakers
  • Mom's steaks (Or as I used to call it, "Chicken")
 
Fine Dining

I've had a lot of food experiences in my life. Some of them were good, some of them were bad. But I've learned a lot from them. The first upscale experience I had was at a restaurant called "Duane's" at the Riverside Mission Inn. I believe it was for my 9th birthday, and I had one of the best Filet Mignons I have ever had. Granted, since that was my first reference for expensive meat cuts, there was no bar to beat. In my adult experience, Duane's holds a special place in my heart, but it's nothing to get your hopes up for.

The first Michelin rated restaurant I ate at was at Citrin and Melisse, a French-New American spot in Santa Monica, California. To this day, I dream of the egg dish my mom and I each had when we visited called "La Egg" It was a beautiful dish of poached eggs with a creamy sauce topped with caviar and served with a flakey crostini.
